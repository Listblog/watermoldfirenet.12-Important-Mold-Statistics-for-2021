11 Important Mold Statistics for 2021

Reports say around 70% of U.S. homes contain a certain level of mold. Mold damages your home structually and threatens the health of everyone living in the home. Here are 12 important statistics about mold and air pollution caused by mold.

Read Post

1. Around 70% of homes contain mold

and 28% of people have mold allergies. In addition, 18% of school buildings contain mold and 26% of hospitals.

2. The annual cost

of illnesses caused by indoor mold is $3.7 billion.

3. The most common indoor molds

are Aspergillus, Penicillium, and Cladosporium. These are called “indicator” molds, they are the first molds to show up and are some of the fastest spreading molds.

4. Mold Problems are more common

than lead paint or asbestos.

5. Black mold is sometimes called toxic mold

althought not all black molds are toxic. In fact, the color of the mold has nothing to do with its level of toxicity.

6. Mold growth can be prevented

by fixing leaks quickly, drying wet structural materials, and maintaining indoor humidity levels between 30%-50%.

7. After a home is damaged by water,

mold can take only 24-48 hours to begin to grow. Time is always the most critical factor in preventing mold growth.

9. Even a slight exposure

to mold can be harmful. And you don't have to have mold sensitivities for your health to be affected. Common symptoms include coughing, itchy eyes, sore throat, or skin rashes.

10. Medical science has no tests

to determine if it is mold that making you sick.
